Stars

Did you know that the North Star, also known as Polaris, is the brightest star in the constellation Ursa Minor?

The hottest known star, WR 104, has a surface temperature of over 200,000 degrees Celsius (360,000 degrees Fahrenheit).

The largest known star, UY Scuti, is about 1,700 times larger than the Sun.

There are estimated to be over 100 billion stars in the Milky Way galaxy alone.
