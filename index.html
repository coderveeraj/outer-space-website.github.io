Solar System

Our solar system consists of the sun and all the objects that orbit around it, including planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and meteoroids. There are eight planets in our solar system, which are divided into two categories: inner planets and outer planets. The inner planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, and the outer planets are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Galaxies

A galaxy is a vast collection of gas, dust, and billions of stars and their solar systems, held together by gravity. There are billions of galaxies in the universe, and each one is unique. The most well-known galaxy is the Milky Way, which is the galaxy that our solar system is part of. Other famous galaxies include the Andromeda Galaxy and the Triangulum Galaxy.

Cosmology

Cosmology is the study of the origin, evolution, and ultimate fate of the universe. It is a branch of astronomy that uses mathematics and physical laws to understand and explain the properties and behavior of the universe on the largest scales. Some of the major questions that cosmologists try to answer include: How did the universe begin? How has it evolved over time? What is the structure of the universe? What is the ultimate fate of the universe?
